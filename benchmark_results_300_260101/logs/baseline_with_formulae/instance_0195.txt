To calculate the corrected QT interval (QTc) using the Bazett formula, we use the following formula:

**QTc Bazett: QTc = QT / sqrt(RR)**

Where:
- **QT** is the QT interval in milliseconds (msec)  
- **RR** is the RR interval in seconds (s), calculated as **60 / HR** (where HR is heart rate in bpm)  

### Given:
- **HR** = 125 bpm  
- **QT** = 403 msec  

### Steps:
1. Calculate the **RR interval**:  
   \[
   RR = \frac{60}{HR} = \frac{60}{125} = 0.48 \text{ seconds}
   \]
2. Take the square root of the RR interval:  
   \[
   \sqrt{RR} = \sqrt{0.48} \approx 0.6928
   \]
3. Divide the QT interval by the square root of RR:  
   \[
   QTc = \frac{403}{0.6928} \approx 581.7 \text{ msec}
   \]
