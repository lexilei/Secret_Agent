Question: Using the Bazett Formula for corrected QT interval, what is the patient's corrected QT interval in terms of msec?

To calculate the corrected QT interval (QTc) using the Bazett formula, we use the following formula:

\[ \text{QTc} = \frac{\text{QT}}{\sqrt{\text{RR}}} \]

Where:
- **QT** is the QT interval in seconds (440 msec = 0.440 sec)
- **RR** is the RR interval in seconds, calculated as \( \frac{60}{\text{HR}} \), where HR is the heart rate in bpm (50 bpm).

### Step-by-Step Calculation:
1. **Calculate RR interval:**
   \[ \text{RR} = \frac{60}{50} = 1.2 \text{ sec} \]

2. **Calculate QTc using Bazett formula:**
   \[ \text{QTc} = \frac{0.440}{\sqrt{1.2}} \]
   \[ \text{QTc} = \frac{0.440}{1.0954} \]
   \[ \text{QTc} \approx 0.4017 \text{ sec} \]
   \[ \text{QTc} \approx 401.7 \text{ msec} \]
