A patient has a heart rate of 102 bpm and a QT interval of 412 msec.

Question: Using the Bazett Formula for corrected QT interval, what is the patient's corrected QT interval in terms of msec?

To calculate the corrected QT interval (QTc) using the Bazett formula, we use the following formula:

\[ \text{QTc} = \frac{\text{QT}}{\sqrt{\text{RR}}} \]

Where:
- **QT** is the QT interval in milliseconds (msec) = **412 msec** (given)
- **RR** is the RR interval in seconds (s), calculated as \( \frac{60}{\text{HR}} \), where HR is the heart rate in beats per minute (bpm) = **102 bpm** (given)

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1. **Calculate the RR interval (in seconds):**
   \[
   \text{RR} = \frac{60}{\text{HR}} = \frac{60}{102} \approx 0.5882 \text{ s}
   \]

2. **Take the square root of the RR interval:**
   \[
   \sqrt{\text{RR}} = \sqrt{0.5882} \approx 0.7669
   \]

3. **Divide the QT interval by the square root of RR to get QTc:**
   \[
   \text{QTc} = \frac{412}{0.7669} \approx 537.2 \text{ msec}
   \]
