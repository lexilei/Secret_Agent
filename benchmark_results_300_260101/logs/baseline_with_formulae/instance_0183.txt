To calculate the corrected QT interval (QTc) using the Bazett formula, we use the following formula:

\[ \text{QTc} = \frac{\text{QT}}{\sqrt{\text{RR}}} \]

Where:
- **QT** is the QT interval in milliseconds (406 msec in this case).
- **RR** is the RR interval in seconds, which is calculated as \( \frac{60}{\text{HR}} \), where HR is the heart rate in beats per minute (150 bpm in this case).

### Step-by-Step Calculation:
1. **Calculate the RR interval (in seconds):**
   \[ \text{RR} = \frac{60}{\text{HR}} = \frac{60}{150} = 0.4 \text{ seconds} \]

2. **Take the square root of the RR interval:**
   \[ \sqrt{\text{RR}} = \sqrt{0.4} \approx 0.6325 \]

3. **Divide the QT interval by the square root of RR:**
   \[ \text{QTc} = \frac{406}{0.6325} \approx 642 \text{ msec} \]
