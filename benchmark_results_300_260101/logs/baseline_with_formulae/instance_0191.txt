To calculate the corrected QT interval (QTc) using the Bazett formula, we use the following formula:

**QTc Bazett: QTc = QT / sqrt(RR)**

Where:
- **QT** is the QT interval in milliseconds (msec)  
- **RR** is the RR interval in seconds (calculated as 60 / heart rate)  

### Given:
- Heart rate (HR) = 108 bpm  
- QT interval = 431 msec  

### Steps:
1. Calculate the RR interval (in seconds):  
   \[ RR = \frac{60}{HR} = \frac{60}{108} \approx 0.5556 \text{ seconds} \]  

2. Take the square root of RR:  
   \[ \sqrt{RR} = \sqrt{0.5556} \approx 0.7454 \]  

3. Divide QT by \(\sqrt{RR}\):  
   \[ QTc = \frac{431}{0.7454} \approx 578.2 \text{ msec} \]
